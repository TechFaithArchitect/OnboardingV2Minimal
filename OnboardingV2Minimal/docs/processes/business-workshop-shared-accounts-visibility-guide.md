# Business Workshop Guide: Shared Accounts, Meetings, and Visibility

Use this guide with Sales Leadership, Revenue Operations, and frontline managers to agree how teams should collaborate on shared customers.

## Why this matters

When multiple teams work the same customer, confusion happens fast unless the business sets clear rules for:
- Who owns the relationship
- Who can see meetings and messages
- What leaders can see
- What must be measured in dashboards

This guide helps you make those decisions before rollout.

## The 3 Decisions the Business Must Make

1. Account coverage rules:
Who works which customers, and when customers can be shared across teams.

2. Visibility rules:
Who can see meetings and communication activity across peers, managers, and leaders.

3. Reporting rules:
What leadership expects to measure and review every week/month.

## Workshop Outcomes

By the end of the session, you should have:
- A clear customer-sharing policy
- A clear collaboration vs privacy policy
- A manager and leadership visibility policy
- A reporting expectation list signed off by leadership
- Named owners for ongoing governance

## Business Questions to Ask

### 1) Customer ownership and sharing
- Can one customer be worked by more than one rep or team?
- If yes, who is the primary owner?
- What can shared reps do: view only, update, create deals?
- Are there customer types that should never be shared?

### 2) Team collaboration and privacy
- Should reps at the same level see each other’s customer meetings?
- Should they see each other’s customer emails/messages?
- Should visibility differ by team type (core team vs specialist team)?
- Where do we want stronger privacy controls?

### 3) Manager and executive visibility
- Should first-line managers see all activity from their teams?
- Should directors and VPs see all activity below them?
- Are there leaders outside direct reporting lines who still need visibility?

### 4) Special and restricted customers
- Which customers require stricter privacy?
- Which meeting types should be limited (for example: executive or legal discussions)?
- Who can approve exceptions?

### 5) Reporting expectations
- What activity metrics must appear in executive dashboards?
- Do we need activity reporting by rep, manager, team, and region?
- How much detail is needed: totals only or detailed trend views?
- What reporting gaps are unacceptable?

### 6) Change management
- What happens when customer coverage changes between teams?
- What happens when a rep leaves the company?
- Who decides and communicates visibility changes?

### 7) Governance and accountability
- Who owns the policy?
- Who approves changes?
- How often are policies reviewed?
- Who handles escalations when teams disagree?

## Four Scenario Exercises

Use these in the workshop and record final decisions.

### Scenario 1: Two reps share one customer
- Should both reps see all meetings?
- Should both reps see all messages?

### Scenario 2: One customer is covered by two teams
- Should both teams see the same full activity history?
- Or only activity directly involving their team members?

### Scenario 3: Rep exits the company
- Who takes over account history and visibility?
- What should remain available to leadership?

### Scenario 4: Strategic/confidential customer
- Which activities stay restricted?
- Which leaders still need access?

## The Key Executive Tradeoff

Ask this directly:

`Do we value broader collaboration more, or stricter activity privacy more?`

- Collaboration-first: faster teamwork, broader visibility, lighter boundaries.
- Privacy-first: tighter controls, reduced sharing, stronger restrictions.
- Most enterprises choose a hybrid based on customer type and team role.

## 12 Executive-Level Questions

1. Can customers be shared across teams?
2. Who is accountable as primary owner when customers are shared?
3. Should same-level reps see each other’s meetings?
4. Should same-level reps see each other’s communication activity?
5. Should managers see all team activity?
6. Should directors and VPs see all subordinate activity?
7. Should specialist/overlay teams see core team activity?
8. Which customer groups need tighter privacy controls?
9. What activity metrics are required in leadership dashboards?
10. What should happen to visibility when teams are realigned?
11. Who owns policy decisions and enforcement?
12. How often should this policy be reviewed?
